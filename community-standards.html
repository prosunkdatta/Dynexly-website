Community Standards

Together we build a space for rigorous research, respect, and open collaboration.

Effective June 1, 2026

Dynexly is a global, youth‑led research collective built on the principles of open science, collaboration, and respect. These Community Standards define the expectations for everyone who participates in our projects, communications, events, and online spaces. By joining Dynexly as a researcher, contributor, or community member, you agree to uphold these standards.

🧬 Our Core Values in Action

🔓 Open First 🔬 Scientific Rigor 🌱 Youth‑Governed 💸 $0 Principle 🛠 Research‑to‑Product 🌍 Global by Design 📊 Empirical Impact

Every interaction within the Dynexly community should reflect these values. We judge contributions on their merit, not on the identity of the contributor.

⚡ Research Integrity

All research conducted under the Dynexly name must adhere to the highest standards of scientific integrity:

  • 🔁 Reproducibility: Code, data, and methodologies must be documented well enough that another researcher can independently reproduce the results.
  • ✅ Honest Reporting: Do not fabricate, falsify, or misrepresent data, results, or citations. Report limitations and uncertainties transparently.
  • 📂 Open Access: All final research outputs must be released under MIT License unless otherwise approved.
  • 🚫 Plagiarism: Never present another's work as your own. Always credit original sources.
  • 👥 Peer Review: Constructive, respectful critique is welcomed. All projects undergo internal review.

🤝 Respectful Collaboration

Dynexly brings together young researchers from diverse backgrounds, countries, and cultures. We are committed to maintaining an environment where everyone can contribute free from harassment, discrimination, or hostility.

  • ❌ Zero Tolerance for Harassment: Harassment of any kind will not be tolerated.
  • 🗣 Inclusive Language: Use language that welcomes everyone. Avoid unexplained jargon.
  • 🧠 Constructive Disagreement: Disagree with ideas, not people.
  • ⭐ Credit Where Due: Authorship follows IEEE guidelines.

📡 Communication Channels

These standards apply to all spaces where Dynexly work occurs, including GitHub repositories, email, social media, video calls, and in‑person events.

©️ Intellectual Property & Open‑Source

By contributing to Dynexly, you agree that your contributions will be under the MIT License, and that you have the right to contribute the material. Dynexly may use, modify, and distribute your contributions with proper attribution.

🛡️ Reporting Violations

If you experience or witness behavior that violates these standards, report it promptly. Reports are handled confidentially. Send details to dynexlylabs@gmail.com. Anonymous reporting is possible, and retaliation is prohibited.

⚖️ Consequences of Violations

Dynexly’s Executive Director, in consultation with team leads, will review reported violations. Possible consequences include:

  • ⚠️ A warning with guidance on corrective behavior.
  • ⏳ Temporary suspension from communication channels and repositories.
  • 🚪 Permanent removal from the Dynexly community.
  • 📢 In cases of serious misconduct, public disclosure may be made to protect the community.

Decisions may be appealed by emailing the Executive Director.

📝 Amendments

These Community Standards may be updated. Proposed changes will be shared with the community for feedback before final adoption.
